Urban Open Space

ParkUp

Taipei City left too many vacant lots but too few open spaces. As a placemaking brand, ParkUp focuses on renovating those neglected leftover spaces into creative urban open spaces. By conducting a series of spatial experiments, ParkUp not only remodels visible sites but reconnects invisible broken relationships between communities and neighborhoods. Little by little, making a better city we can all share. ParkUp remodels idle spaces with 3 principles: 1. Under 165 sqm-To plan only for small idle spaces. 2. Inclusive Design-To ensure accessibility for all. 3. Localization-To discover the real needs of space users. We also devised ParkUp Tools to facilitate placemaking. It consists of wood columns and a series of metal products that can be intuitively assembled. The modular assembling system makes it easy to maintain, which allows for replacing any single damaged unit without retiring the whole set.

Here's what I did in this project: I am responsible for designing the application of the modular structure of ParkUp SYC, which is the third space renovation project in the ParkUp series. It is located in the atrium square of Song Yan Court (SYC), with 156m2. Considering that this base is located in Songshan Cultural and Creative Park, there is the Not Just Library next to it, so the open space is mainly for reading and rest. Due to the hot weather in Taiwan, a detachable canopy was designed above the structure to extend the time users stay there. In addition, layers and structures of different heights are also designed to meet the needs of standing, sitting and lying down. It is suitable for a group of people to communicate here.
