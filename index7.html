Ron Museum

Ron Museum, a renovation of a street food shop that sells Douhua. The owner is the third generation, and the shop in the grandfather's generation was located under the banyan tree in the military village, so they hoped the space design could create a similar image. Since the store is only 68m2, we focused the design on local materials and did a lot of research and dyeing tests. First, the wood is made of Taiwan Southern Pine, and the bar facade is spliced with the natural pattern of the section. Second, the paint on the exterior wall is blended with waste rice husks and mineral paint, echoing the Douhua ingredients.
